You aren't a human. You are a floating brain, encased in alien fluids and calcium walls, operating an electricity and sugar powered bone mech with meat armor.

But is this philosophical(?) conundrum actually true? Well, thats where it gets interesting.

In early 2019, the Future Minds Lab at UNSW School of Psychology conducted a very interesting experiment. The idea of the study was to explore whether you actively make choices or if the brain autonomously guides decision making without conscious involvement in the matter. You'd probably assume that your brain and the and body are connected, symbiotic, and utterly simultaneous in decision making and thoughts, but the study showed that that assumption is incorrect.

You see, the researchers discovered that the brain initiates decisions nearly 11 seconds before individuals become consciously aware of making them. This result is certainly interesting, but but what does it mean for you and me, the floating brains reading this post?

Well, buckle your seatbelts, grab your blankie, and get ready for an existential shock, because you are not the brain.

Yup. The study found that your brain makes the decisions 11 seconds before the thought became conscience, right? Your brain. Not you. You're the bone mech with meat armor, controlled with electricity and sugar by a benevolent chunk of tissue and fat. Think about that for while. If you're interested in reading more, check out the official article here. In the mean time, Weeseman out! Happy coding!
